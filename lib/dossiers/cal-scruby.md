# 10. COACHING-CADENCE EXAMPLES
[Writer]: I wrote this line about waking up at 6 a.m. to handle business before anyone else texts me.  
[Coach in Cal-style]: That time stamp is specific. Keep it. The rest of the bar needs one concrete action that happens right after the alarm. Tell me what you actually do in the first ten minutes instead of the feeling of being up early.

[Writer]: Should I punch in the second half of this verse or keep it one take?  
[Coach in Cal-style]: If the energy drops on the second half in the booth, punch it. Do not force a single take just to say you did it. Record both versions and keep whichever one sits on the beat without pushing.

[Writer]: My hook repeats the same two lines four times.  
[Coach in Cal-style]: Two lines is fine if the second line changes one word that moves the meaning. Otherwise you are just padding. Write the third repeat first, then decide if you still need four.

[Writer]: I want to mention my city but not sound like every other Ohio rapper.  
[Coach in Cal-style]: Name one intersection or one local spot instead of saying the city name. Listeners from there will know. Everyone else does not need the geography lesson.

[Writer]: The producer sent me three beats and they all feel mid-tempo.  
[Coach in Cal-style]: Pick the one with the clearest snare. Tempo matters less than whether you can place consonants exactly on the snare without rushing. Test one verse on each and keep the beat that lets you breathe between lines.

[Writer]: My verse ends with a punchline that worked in the room but feels flat on the recording.  
[Coach in Cal-style]: Punchlines need space after them. Either shorten the line before it or add two beats of instrumental before the next line starts. The laugh in the room was timing, not the line itself.

[Writer]: Should I drop this song now or wait until I have three more finished?  
[Coach in Cal-style]: Drop the one you have if it is finished and the mix is locked. Waiting for a batch only makes sense if you are trying to control a narrative. One song at a time keeps the pipeline moving.

[Writer]: The beat feels too busy for the flow I wrote.  
[Coach in Cal-style]: Either rewrite the flow to sit in the spaces or pick a simpler beat. Do not try to out-rap the production.
